# Power Valley homepage reference concepts

These images are visual reference material for the public homepage experience. They are intended to give implementation agents a concrete standard for composition, content density, photography, hierarchy and overall production quality.

They are **directional references, not pixel-perfect specifications**. The implemented homepage must remain responsive, accessible, performant and compatible with reduced-motion preferences under [`docs/18-product-experience-and-visual-design.md`](../../18-product-experience-and-visual-design.md).

## Reference concepts

### 1. Search-first local discovery

![Search-first local discovery homepage](01-search-first-local-discovery.jpg)

Use as a reference for:

- A strong photographic Valleys hero.
- Immediate two-part search and location controls.
- Featured businesses, weekend events and area discovery.
- A clear demonstration of the generated business website proposition.
- Warm mineral surfaces, deep green accents and editorial typography.

### 2. Editorial community discovery

![Editorial community discovery homepage](02-editorial-community-discovery.jpg)

Use as a reference for:

- A bright editorial treatment combining local life and useful product content.
- Real photography and believable information throughout the page.
- Nearby businesses, weekend events, local guides and town cards.
- A spacious three-column discovery section and strong business call to action.
- A premium local publication feel without becoming a news portal.

### 3. Map-led product discovery

![Map-led product discovery homepage](03-map-led-product-discovery.jpg)

Use as a reference for:

- A product-led search and map opening experience.
- Clear tabs for businesses, events, places and guides.
- Explaining how one business profile becomes search visibility, listings and a generated website.
- Useful category, business and event modules rather than decorative placeholders.
- A clean, application-quality interface with restrained green styling.

## Implementation guidance

The final design may combine the strongest elements from all three concepts. In particular, preserve:

- Search as the primary first-screen interaction.
- Authentic local photography and recognisable Valleys context.
- Properly populated cards and modules with realistic content structure.
- Clear resident discovery and business pathways.
- The generated one-page business website as a flagship proposition.
- Strong spacing, grid alignment and readable typography.
- No overlapping panels, broken card layouts, empty placeholder blocks or generic AI-dashboard styling.

The business names, event dates, ratings, statistics, addresses, testimonials and map results shown in the images are fictional design content. They must not be presented as verified production data. Replace them with approved seed data or clearly identified fictional demonstration data during implementation.

The words **Power Valley** in these visual concepts are a working-name treatment only. Adding these assets does not independently change the repository's canonical brand decision or the current **OurValleys** working name.

These repository copies are compressed implementation-reference images derived from the selected full-page renders.
