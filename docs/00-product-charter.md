# Product Charter

## 1. Product definition

OurValleys is an independent local discovery and business platform for Rhondda Cynon Taf.

It brings together local businesses, residents, community organisations, events, guides, offers and selected local updates through one shared location-based system. Its flagship product is an automatically generated website for each participating business.

A business enters and maintains its information once. That information can then appear across:

- Its public business website.
- Search and category results.
- Town and village pages.
- Offers and events.
- Relevant guides and collections.
- Resident saved lists and feeds.
- Future jobs, marketplace and property modules.

## 2. Purpose

Make it easier to live, work, shop, visit and do business locally while giving small organisations an affordable, maintainable digital presence.

## 3. Public propositions

### Residents

> Find what is useful, available and happening near you.

### Businesses

> Create your local business profile once and receive a website, discoverability and tools that help customers find and contact you.

### Community organisations

> Publish your activities, events and opportunities without maintaining several disconnected local listings.

## 4. Problem statement

Local information is fragmented across search engines, social groups, council pages, national platforms, individual business profiles and ageing websites. Residents repeatedly search several places to answer simple local questions.

Small businesses face the reverse problem. They are expected to maintain the same details across websites, social platforms, directories, booking tools and advertising services. Many depend on a social profile alone, have an outdated website or have no manageable website at all.

OurValleys solves this by creating a shared structured local information system rather than another static directory.

## 5. Strategic differentiation

The platform is not primarily differentiated by having listings, articles or events. Those are widely available elsewhere.

The defensible combination is:

1. A genuinely useful generated business website.
2. One business record reused throughout the platform.
3. Strong town, village and distance-based discovery.
4. Resident content and business tools within one system.
5. A credible free-to-paid value path.
6. Local trust, verification and editorial transparency.

## 6. Geographic scope

### Launch area

Rhondda Cynon Taf.

### Geographic structure

1. Region.
2. Valley or local area.
3. Town.
4. Village or neighbourhood.
5. Postcode.
6. Optional service radius.

The platform must represent the Rhondda Fawr, Rhondda Fach, Cynon, Taff and Ely valleys, together with communities that do not fit neatly into a valley label.

### Expansion rule

A wider “Valleys” identity may support future expansion, but public coverage claims must match actual content density. Expansion should happen town by town after the platform is meaningfully useful within RCT.

## 7. Primary audiences

### Residents

People seeking businesses, trades, food, shopping, events, offers, jobs, property, community information, recommendations and local guidance.

### Local businesses

Sole traders, shops, tradespeople, hospitality, venues, professional services, home-based businesses, online businesses, tourism providers and property-related services.

### Community organisations

Charities, clubs, schools, community centres, churches and chapels, social enterprises, support organisations and volunteer groups.

### Contributors

Local writers, photographers, historians, community reporters, students and subject specialists.

### Visitors

People looking for places to stay, eat, visit and attend while in the area.

## 8. Product principles

### Local first

Every feature should become more useful because it understands place, distance and community context.

### Enter once, use everywhere

Businesses and organisations should not repeat the same updates in separate modules.

### Useful free product

The free business page must deliver genuine value and must not be a deliberately broken teaser.

### Paid value, not artificial restriction

Subscriptions should unlock measurable commercial tools, capacity or convenience.

### Trust before growth

Verification, reporting, transparent advertising, corrections and fair moderation take priority over inflated numbers.

### Mobile first

Core discovery, enquiries and business updates must work well on ordinary mobile connections and devices.

### Bilingual-ready

Content models and interface architecture must support English and Welsh even where launch content is initially incomplete in one language.

### Accessible by design

Components and generated templates should target WCAG 2.2 AA from the beginning.

### Independent and transparent

OurValleys must not imply that it is operated by or officially endorsed by a council, public body or publisher without a formal relationship.

### Density before expansion

Useful local coverage in a smaller area is more valuable than empty pages across a larger region.

## 9. Phase 1 outcome

Phase 1 succeeds when:

- A business can create, verify, preview and publish a useful one-page website without developer assistance.
- A resident can find a relevant nearby business or event and make contact.
- Town pages contain current, useful and connected content.
- Administrators can review claims, reports and content changes.
- The platform records meaningful connections rather than only page views.
- Safety-sensitive future modules remain unavailable until their controls are ready.

## 10. Phase 1 inclusions

- Resident and business accounts.
- Business onboarding.
- Business claiming and basic verification.
- Generated one-page business websites.
- Services, opening hours, service areas and media.
- Search, categories and filters.
- Town and village pages.
- Events.
- Local guides and controlled updates.
- Contact and quotation enquiries.
- Saved businesses and events.
- Business and administration dashboards.
- Reporting, moderation and audit foundations.
- Basic product and business analytics.

## 11. Explicit Phase 1 exclusions

- Managed marketplace payments.
- Full open forum.
- Unrestricted direct messaging.
- Native mobile applications.
- Full property intermediary services.
- Food delivery.
- Bespoke free website design.
- Platform-wide loyalty currency.
- Complex ecommerce.
- Automated copying or rewriting of third-party news.
- Expansion across every South Wales valley.

## 12. Long-term modules

Subject to evidence, moderation capacity and legal readiness:

- Offers and loyalty.
- Reviews.
- Jobs and volunteering.
- Marketplace.
- Community questions and groups.
- Property advertising.
- Event ticketing.
- Gift vouchers.
- Booking and payment services.
- Business CRM and marketing automation.
- Geographic expansion.

## 13. North-star measure

**Successful local connections created each month.**

Qualifying connections may include:

- Business enquiry.
- Quote request.
- Booking or booking handoff.
- Event registration.
- Job application.
- Marketplace enquiry.
- Property enquiry.
- Volunteer registration.
- Confirmed use of a useful local answer.

Each event type must be defined precisely enough to prevent double counting.

## 14. Product non-goals

OurValleys is not intended to:

- Replace official council information.
- Present every user contribution as verified news.
- Act as an estate agent during the initial property phase.
- Guarantee the quality of every listed business.
- Become a general social network disconnected from local utility.
- Make paid placement indistinguishable from ordinary results.
- Gather personal data merely because it may become useful later.

## 15. Decision test for new features

A proposed feature should not enter the roadmap unless it answers all of the following:

1. Which local user problem does it solve?
2. Why is OurValleys better placed to solve it than a generic platform?
3. Does it strengthen the business-resident growth loop?
4. What moderation, privacy and legal duties does it introduce?
5. What administrative tools are required?
6. How will success be measured?
7. What existing work will be delayed by adding it?

## 16. Current product hypothesis

The primary hypothesis is that a useful free business website and local discovery presence will attract enough businesses to create valuable resident content. Once businesses receive measurable enquiries and visibility, a meaningful proportion will pay for improved domains, presentation, analytics, enquiry handling, booking, marketing and commerce tools.

This hypothesis must be validated with real businesses before substantial engineering investment.
