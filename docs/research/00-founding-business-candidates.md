# Founding Business Candidate Research

## Purpose

This research creates a safe starting pool for later founding-business validation. It does **not** authorise outreach and it does not create public business profiles.

The current candidate file is:

`data/research/founding-business-candidates.csv`

## Method

- Use public local directory and visitor-economy sources.
- Capture only business name, town, broad category, source URL and a short research reason.
- Do not copy descriptions, photographs, personal email addresses or telephone numbers.
- Mark every row `research_only_revalidate_before_contact`.
- Re-check that the business is still trading and that the source is current before any contact.
- Obtain the product owner's bounded approval before sending external outreach in their name.

## Initial finding

Treorchy provides a useful concentrated pilot cluster because public sources show independent businesses across retail, hospitality, practical services, beauty and professional services within one town.

That concentration suits the product strategy of building local density before broad geographic coverage.

## What this dataset proves—and does not prove

It proves that a varied pool of public candidate organisations can be assembled without collecting unnecessary personal data.

It does not prove:

- That a business is still operating.
- That its details are accurate.
- That it wants an OurValleys page.
- That copied content may be reused.
- That inclusion is an endorsement.
- That the business has been verified.

## Next autonomous action

Before outreach, prepare a revalidation pass that records:

- Current trading evidence.
- Official business-controlled URL or social presence where available.
- Category fit.
- Whether a generated one-page site appears to solve a visible gap.
- Duplicate risk.

External contact remains an approval gate.
