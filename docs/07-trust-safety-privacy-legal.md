# Trust, Safety, Privacy and Legal Readiness

## 1. Purpose and status

OurValleys will publish business information and, in later phases, may allow users to submit reviews, listings, comments, messages and other content. Trust and safety therefore form part of the product architecture, not an after-launch policy exercise.

This document is an operational planning framework. It is not legal advice. Appropriate UK legal and data-protection review is required before public launch and before introducing higher-risk modules.

## 2. Core rules

- Do not launch user-generated content without reporting, moderation, complaints and audit controls.
- Do not call a business “verified” without explaining exactly what was checked.
- Do not mix sponsored and editorial content without clear disclosure.
- Do not collect personal data without a defined purpose, lawful basis and retention rule.
- Do not expose private home addresses or verification evidence through public interfaces.
- Do not present OurValleys as an official council or public-body service.
- Do not expand a property module from advertising into intermediary activity without fresh legal review.
- Do not treat a terms page as a substitute for effective systems and processes.

## 3. Service mapping

Before launch, identify each service component and whether users can encounter content generated by another user.

Potential user-to-user components include:

- Business-created public pages.
- Event submissions.
- Reviews.
- Marketplace listings.
- Community posts and comments.
- User-uploaded images.
- Public questions and answers.
- Messaging where messages may be reported or moderated.

Search functionality may also require separate consideration under the UK online safety regime depending on its design and scope.

Maintain a service map showing:

- Feature.
- User types.
- Content types.
- Public or private encounter routes.
- Children’s likely access.
- Moderation method.
- Reporting route.
- Record owner.
- Launch date and material changes.

## 4. Online Safety Act readiness

Ofcom’s current guidance states that in-scope user-to-user and search services must assess illegal-content risks, maintain records, implement proportionate protections and operate reporting and complaints processes. Current guidance also requires a children’s access assessment for relevant Part 3 services. Ofcom states that a newly launched in-scope service has three months to complete an illegal-content risk assessment and must complete one before a significant service change.

OurValleys should not wait until the end of that period. Complete the assessment before enabling public user-generated content so controls can be built into the release.

### Required pre-launch work

- Use Ofcom’s regulation checker or obtain specialist advice on scope.
- Map every user-to-user and search feature.
- Complete and record the illegal-content risk assessment.
- Complete and record the children’s access assessment.
- If required, complete the children’s risk assessment.
- Document safety measures and responsible owners.
- Publish accurate terms explaining prohibited content and enforcement.
- Provide an easy reporting route.
- Provide complaints and appeal processes.
- Define urgent escalation and evidence-preservation procedures.
- Review assessments after significant changes and on the required cycle.

### Product implications

Every user-generated content entity needs:

- Author and creation record.
- Publication state.
- Report action.
- Visibility restriction.
- Moderator decision record.
- Appeal link where applicable.
- Rate and abuse controls.
- Retention and deletion rules.

## 5. Children and age considerations

OurValleys is a general local service and may be attractive to young people seeking events, jobs, activities or marketplace items.

Before launch:

- Determine whether children are likely to access each service.
- Avoid collecting children’s data unnecessarily.
- Do not introduce private adult-to-child messaging casually.
- Consider whether particular categories or listings require age restrictions.
- Design reporting and blocking controls appropriate to the risk.
- Avoid manipulative engagement patterns.
- Review marketplace, jobs, comments and messaging separately before launch.

The platform should not claim to be “not for children” as a substitute for assessing actual likely access.

## 6. Moderation model

## 6.1 Moderation layers

1. **Prevention:** structured fields, upload controls, limits and clear rules.
2. **Detection:** automated signals, user reports and staff review.
3. **Action:** reject, hide, label, restrict, suspend or escalate.
4. **Communication:** notify affected users appropriately.
5. **Appeal:** provide review where policy or law requires it.
6. **Learning:** analyse recurring abuse and update the risk assessment.

## 6.2 Report categories

Initial controlled categories may include:

- Scam or fraud.
- False or misleading business information.
- Impersonation.
- Harassment or threats.
- Hate or discriminatory content.
- Illegal goods or services.
- Sexual or child-safety concern.
- Privacy or personal information.
- Intellectual property concern.
- Unsafe event or listing.
- Spam.
- Incorrect or outdated information.
- Other legal concern.

High-risk categories must enter a priority workflow rather than a general support inbox.

## 6.3 Moderator requirements

- Written policy basis.
- Least access necessary.
- Training and escalation route.
- Decision templates that allow contextual reasoning.
- Audit logs.
- Conflict-of-interest process for local disputes.
- Welfare consideration for staff handling harmful material.
- Quality review of significant decisions.

## 7. Business verification

Verification must be specific and understandable.

Possible checks:

- Email verified.
- Telephone verified.
- Business ownership checked.
- Address checked.
- Organisation status checked.
- Professional qualification checked.

A single generic shield can mislead users into assuming more has been checked than is true.

### Verification display rules

- Badge text states the check.
- Explanation page defines method and limitations.
- Expiring checks display current status.
- A subscription never grants verification automatically.
- Rejected or expired evidence is not displayed as valid.
- High-risk categories may require additional checks before publication.

## 8. Reviews

Reviews should be deferred until the review system can comply with current UK consumer-protection requirements.

The CMA publishes specific guidance for businesses and online review sites following the fake-review provisions introduced in April 2025. OurValleys will need reasonable and proportionate processes to prevent and remove fake or concealed incentivised reviews and to avoid presenting review information misleadingly.

### Review launch requirements

- Review eligibility policy.
- Conflict-of-interest prohibition.
- Incentive disclosure and controls.
- Verified-enquiry or transaction indicator where supportable.
- Business response.
- User reporting.
- Detection of bursts, duplicates and coordinated activity.
- Moderation and appeal.
- Fair presentation of positive and negative reviews.
- No suppression based solely on negativity.
- Published explanation of how rankings and averages work.
- Retention of evidence proportionate to complaint needs.

### Prohibited product shortcuts

- Importing unverified star ratings without provenance.
- Allowing businesses to delete negative reviews directly.
- Selling higher ratings.
- Hiding incentivised status.
- Describing every submitted review as verified.

## 9. Advertising, sponsorship and affiliate content

Advertising must be obviously identifiable.

Requirements:

- Use clear labels such as “Sponsored” or “Advertisement”.
- Keep a sponsorship record in the data model.
- Disclose affiliate relationships near relevant content.
- Separate organic search logic from paid placement.
- Prevent business subscriptions from purchasing undisclosed editorial recommendation.
- Keep editorial inclusion criteria documented.
- Allow users to understand why a sponsored result appears.

Do not rely on vague labels such as “Featured” where the commercial nature would not be obvious.

## 10. Editorial content, corrections and complaints

Every published item should identify its content type:

- Original editorial.
- Community submission.
- Business update.
- Press release.
- Opinion.
- Sponsored content.
- Public information.

Operational requirements:

- Named accountable editor.
- Source and evidence notes for contentious material.
- Pre-publication review for legal risk.
- Corrections route.
- Complaint tracking.
- Material correction notice with date.
- Right-of-reply consideration.
- Removal and preservation process for disputes.

Do not scrape and rewrite copyrighted local news as a content strategy.

## 11. Privacy and data protection

## 11.1 Data inventory

Maintain a record of processing covering:

- User accounts.
- Business memberships.
- Public business information.
- Private verification evidence.
- Enquiries.
- Saved content and location preferences.
- Analytics.
- Email and notifications.
- Moderation and reports.
- Billing and subscriptions.
- Support requests.

For each activity record:

- Purpose.
- Data categories.
- Data subjects.
- Lawful basis.
- Source.
- Recipients and processors.
- International transfer mechanism where relevant.
- Retention.
- Security controls.
- Data-subject rights handling.

## 11.2 Data minimisation

Do not require:

- Date of birth for ordinary browsing or business setup without a real need.
- Precise resident home address for location preference.
- Sensitive personal information in general enquiry forms.
- Public display of a home-based business address.
- Copies of identity evidence after the verification purpose has ended unless retention is justified.

## 11.3 Public business data

Named sole traders and business contacts may still be personal data. Public availability elsewhere does not remove data-protection obligations.

- Explain the source of platform-created profiles.
- Provide correction and objection routes.
- Distinguish public contact data from private account data.
- Do not automatically reuse a public email for unrelated marketing.

## 11.4 Enquiries

- Show an appropriate privacy notice at collection.
- Collect only fields needed for the enquiry.
- Protect against spam and abuse.
- Define whether OurValleys or the business is controller for each stage.
- Set retention rules.
- Allow deletion or restriction where appropriate.
- Avoid sensitive free-text prompts.

## 11.5 Cookies and analytics

- Categorise cookies and similar technologies.
- Avoid non-essential tracking before valid choice where consent is required.
- Make rejection as understandable as acceptance.
- Keep analytics event properties allowlisted.
- Do not send private enquiry content or verification evidence to analytics.
- Record consent state and update it when preferences change.

## 11.6 Data rights

Prepare processes for:

- Access.
- Correction.
- Deletion.
- Restriction.
- Objection.
- Portability where applicable.
- Consent withdrawal.
- Complaints.

Account self-service can help but must not be the only route.

## 11.7 Security incidents

Maintain:

- Incident response roles.
- Severity definitions.
- Containment steps.
- Evidence and decision log.
- Processor contact routes.
- Regulatory notification assessment.
- User communication process.
- Post-incident review.

## 12. Electronic marketing

Current ICO guidance distinguishes corporate subscribers from sole traders and certain partnerships, which may be treated similarly to individuals under PECR rules. Marketing workflows must therefore recognise recipient type and communication channel.

Requirements:

- Do not assume all business email addresses can receive unrestricted marketing.
- Record source, purpose and preference.
- Provide identification and an effective opt-out.
- Maintain suppression lists.
- Separate transactional account messages from promotional campaigns.
- Review direct outreach scripts and data sources before large-scale business acquisition campaigns.

## 13. Property module boundary

Current HMRC guidance recognises an exemption for a private-sales property portal that only publishes adverts and enables sellers and buyers to contact one another, provided it does nothing else within the general definition of estate agency work.

Initial property rules should therefore be:

- Advertising only.
- Advertiser controls the listing.
- Buyer contacts advertiser directly.
- OurValleys does not negotiate price.
- OurValleys does not arrange viewings on behalf of the seller.
- OurValleys does not handle deposits.
- OurValleys does not advise either party on completing the transaction.
- Public wording does not describe OurValleys as the estate agent.

Before any expansion beyond this model, obtain specialist advice and review anti-money-laundering, estate-agency and consumer-protection obligations.

## 14. Marketplace boundaries

Before marketplace launch:

- Define prohibited items.
- Define business-seller identification.
- Provide scam warnings.
- Avoid exposing exact private collection addresses publicly.
- Define process for illegal goods reports.
- Rate-limit contact and listing creation.
- Decide whether minors may list or contact sellers.
- Decide whether OurValleys holds money; MVP recommendation is no.
- Plan dispute and law-enforcement request handling.

## 15. Accessibility

Target WCAG 2.2 AA for public pages, dashboards and generated business templates.

Required programme:

- Automated checks.
- Keyboard testing.
- Screen-reader spot checks.
- Zoom and reflow testing.
- Accessible error handling.
- Caption and text-alternative processes.
- Accessibility statement.
- User feedback route.
- Remediation ownership.

Template customisation must not allow businesses to override essential accessibility protections.

## 16. Welsh language readiness

OurValleys is not automatically subject to the Welsh Language Standards merely because it operates in Wales. Duties depend on the statutory framework and the organisations to which standards are applied.

Regardless, bilingual support is a core product and trust objective:

- Separate Welsh and English fields.
- Interface language preference.
- Welsh-speaking business attribute.
- Human review for important platform copy.
- Clear indication when only one language version is supplied.
- No false claim that automated translation is authoritative.

## 17. Intellectual property and media

- Require users to confirm rights to uploaded media.
- Record credits and licence basis where needed.
- Provide an intellectual-property complaint route.
- Avoid downloading business images from third-party sites without permission.
- Remove unnecessary image metadata from public derivatives.
- Do not use uploaded business content to train unrelated models without a clear lawful and contractual basis.
