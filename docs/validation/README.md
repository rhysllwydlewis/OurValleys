# OurValleys Validation Workspace

## Purpose

This directory contains the materials needed to validate the business and resident propositions without requiring the product owner to design the research process.

The agent-led workflow should prepare candidates, scripts, examples, scoring and summaries. Contacting real people remains an external-action approval gate: agents may draft and organise outreach but must not send messages in the product owner’s name without explicit approval or a connected authorised communication workflow.

## Validation questions

1. Do local businesses have a meaningful problem with websites, local visibility or fragmented digital information?
2. Does a generated one-page website solve enough of that problem to motivate setup and maintenance?
3. Which business categories benefit most?
4. Which structured fields are difficult, unnecessary or missing?
5. Which additional tools create genuine willingness to pay?
6. What support effort is required to publish and maintain a page?
7. Do residents understand and value combined local search, events and guides?

## Files

- `business-interview-guide.md` — consistent interview script and note-taking structure.
- `founding-business-intake.md` — pilot onboarding information and content checklist.
- `outreach-message.md` — prepared outreach wording requiring approval before sending.
- `business-validation-scorecard.csv` — structured scoring for each interview.

## Evidence standard

Do not count polite interest as validation.

Stronger evidence includes:

- The business agrees to complete real onboarding.
- The business supplies its real information and media.
- The business publishes or approves a sample page.
- The owner returns to update information later.
- The business agrees to a pilot condition or price after seeing the product.
- A resident successfully uses the platform to find or contact something local.

## Sample targets

The first business sample should include at least:

- Four trades or home-service businesses.
- Four food, drink or hospitality businesses.
- Four retail businesses.
- Four beauty, wellbeing or appointment businesses.
- Four professional-service businesses.
- Two community organisations.

Include a mix of:

- No current website.
- Social-media-only presence.
- Old or difficult-to-update website.
- Modern website already in use.
- Home-based and public-premises businesses.
- Sole traders and small teams.

## Privacy and storage

- Store only information needed for the research.
- Tell participants how notes will be used.
- Do not commit private contact lists, recordings or identifiable interview notes to this public repository.
- Use participant codes in repository summaries.
- Record consent before any recording.
- Delete raw contact and interview data according to the agreed validation retention period.

## Agent workflow

1. Build a candidate list from lawful public sources in an appropriate private workspace.
2. Deduplicate and categorise candidates.
3. Prepare a small outreach batch and recommendation.
4. Request one bounded approval before sending externally.
5. Record interviews using participant codes.
6. Update the scorecard and evidence summary.
7. Revise product documents when repeated evidence contradicts an assumption.

## Exit recommendation

Proceed into a broader pilot when:

- At least ten businesses across several categories agree to build or approve a real page.
- At least six complete the structured intake without excessive intervention.
- Several identify a repeated problem the product clearly solves.
- At least one paid capability has evidence beyond a vague preference.
- No major trust, maintenance or support problem makes the proposition implausible.
