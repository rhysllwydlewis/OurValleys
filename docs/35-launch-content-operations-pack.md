# Launch Content Operations Pack

## Purpose

This pack turns the launch-content requirements in issue #7 and `docs/08-content-locations-localisation.md` into reusable, privacy-safe working templates. It does not invent real businesses, events, endorsements or local facts. Real records must be sourced, verified and stored in an appropriate private operational system before publication.

## Operating principles

- Accuracy before volume.
- Use owner-supplied or independently verified factual information.
- Keep private contact and research notes out of the public repository.
- Record the source, rights basis, owner and review date for every publishable record.
- Do not copy third-party descriptions, articles or images.
- Mark fictional examples clearly and never publish them as real listings.
- Hide thin place pages until they meet the minimum useful-content threshold.

## Founding-business intake template

### Record control

- Internal reference
- Intake date
- Intake facilitator
- Business owner or authorised representative confirmed
- Consent to store supplied information
- Consent to publish selected information

### Public business facts

- Trading name
- Registered or legal name, when relevant and approved for publication
- Primary category
- Secondary categories
- Short factual summary
- Core services
- Public telephone
- Public email
- Public website
- Public social profiles
- Customer-facing address or service-area model
- Opening hours
- Booking or enquiry method
- Accessibility information
- Welsh-language service availability

### Generated website content

- Business story in the owner’s own words
- Frequently asked questions
- Areas served
- Calls to action
- Preferred visual tone
- Approved testimonials with source and permission
- Claims requiring evidence
- Content that must remain private

### Media intake

For every image or video capture:

- File reference
- Rights holder
- Rights basis: owner supplied, commissioned, licensed or other documented permission
- Permission recorded date
- Required credit
- Alt text
- Related business, place or service
- Capture date, where useful
- Campaign or expiry date, where relevant
- Faces or identifiable people present
- Additional consent required

### Commercial research

Store research evidence separately from public copy.

- Current website or digital-presence pain points
- Assisted-onboarding time
- Most valuable proposed feature
- Least valuable proposed feature
- Future paid-tool interest
- Price-response notes without presenting unvalidated prices as settled
- Pilot interest status
- Follow-up permission

## Business content completeness checklist

A profile is launch-useful only when it has:

- a verified trading name;
- a category and at least one specific service;
- a valid public contact route;
- a location or service area;
- opening or availability information when relevant;
- a factual description approved by the business or supported by a recorded source;
- media rights recorded for every published asset;
- an owner and next review date;
- a correction, claim or removal route;
- no private residential address exposed without explicit business-purpose approval.

### Minimum publication threshold

A town page should not be indexable until it has an accurate introduction, several useful businesses across more than one category, at least one current or recurring content source, nearby-place links, and an honest statement of developing coverage.

## Content-source and rights fields

Every content record should support:

- `source_type`
- `source_reference`
- `source_checked_at`
- `content_owner`
- `review_owner`
- `rights_basis`
- `rights_evidence_reference`
- `credit_text`
- `alt_text`
- `language_original`
- `translation_source`
- `translation_review_status`
- `commercial_relationship`
- `last_reviewed_at`
- `next_review_at`
- `expires_at`
- `correction_status`

Private evidence references should point to a controlled operational location rather than embedding personal data or confidential correspondence in public source files.

## Review cadence

| Content type                         | Default review rule                                                                    |
| ------------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| One-off event                        | Confirm before publication, recheck near the date, archive automatically after it ends |
| Recurring event                      | Reconfirm schedule at least every three months                                         |
| Business opening hours               | Business-managed confirmation or six-month review                                      |
| Business services and contact routes | Review every six to twelve months                                                      |
| Town practical information           | Review every six months                                                                |
| Evergreen guide                      | Review every twelve months or when a cited source changes                              |
| Current or seasonal guide            | Explicit expiry date required                                                          |
| Media permission                     | Recheck when the recorded licence, campaign or permission expires                      |

A stale-content process should flag records for review and suppress unsafe time-sensitive claims; it should not silently delete source evidence.

## Private launch working registers

The following real-world registers must be maintained outside the public repository:

- target founding businesses;
- named interview participants and contact details;
- event leads with organiser contact information;
- unpublished source correspondence;
- consent and media-rights evidence;
- commercially sensitive research notes.

The public repository may contain schemas, blank templates and clearly fictional examples only.

## Launch threshold proposal

Before broad promotion of a focus area, require:

- 75–100 useful business profiles across priority categories for the overall launch set;
- several useful businesses across more than one category on every promoted focus-town page;
- at least 30 confirmed current events across the launch set;
- 15–20 useful, reviewed guide briefs or published guides;
- rights-confirmed media or deliberate placeholders;
- named operational owners for business quality, events, town coverage, editorial review, Welsh-language quality, media rights, corrections and stale-content review;
- no promoted empty module and no mass-indexed thin place page.

These are quality gates, not permission to publish unverified information merely to reach a number.
